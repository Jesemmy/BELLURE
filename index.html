    What is Care Home?






    Care homes, also known as adult family homes or personal care homes, provide a unique type of care for seniors and those with disabilities. These homes offer a smaller, more personalized living environment where residents receive around-the-clock care from trained and licensed caregivers.

    Care homes can be an alternative to larger nursing homes or assisted living facilities, and provide a family-style atmosphere where residents can receive a higher level of attention and care from a smaller team of staff and caregivers.

    In many cases, care homes are able to provide more individualized care, as they are better able to tailor the care plan to the specific needs and preferences of each resident. One of the key benefits of care homes is that they are designed to provide a more home-like environment, with fewer residents than larger facilities.

    Residents often share common living spaces, such as living rooms or dining areas, thereby creating a sense of community among residents. Additionally, care homes are often located in residential areas, making it easier for residents to maintain a sense of connection to their local community. In terms of care, care homes provide a wide range of services depending on the needs of the resident. Caregivers are trained to provide personal care, such as bathing, dressing, and grooming, as well as medical care such as medication management and wound care. Many care homes also offer therapeutic services, such as physical therapy, occupational therapy, and speech therapy.
    Overall, care homes offer a unique model of care that can be an excellent option for seniors and those with disabilities who prefer a smaller, more personalized setting in which to receive care. If you or a loved one is considering a care home as a care option, it's important to do your research and visit the home in person to ensure it meets your needs and expectations.

    Type of Care Home


    It is of importance for you to be informed and guided of the types of care home so as to suit the aim for proper care and management of your loved one. The two major type of care homes are;
    - Residential Care Homes : These are homes that provide accommodation, meals and personal care service for individuals who require assistance with daily living activities, but do not require medical attention.

    - Nursing Homes : Here, round-the-clock medical care services are usually offered; including medication administration, wound care, and physical therapy,to individuals with medical needs. This implies nursing homes can offer a higher level of care, most especially for people with physical disabilities who struggle significantly with daily living and people with long-term conditions which require comprehensive care.

    Other types of care homes include:

    - Dementia Care Homes: As the name suggests, these homes are specifically designed for individuals with dementia or other cognitive impairments. They provide specialized care services and activities to support the unique needs of dementia patients.

    - Assisted living communities: These communities offer a mix of independent living and personal care services for residents. They often have apartment-style living spaces and varying levels of assistance for daily living activities.

    - Hospice care Homes: These homes provide end-of-life care services to individuals who have a terminal illness and require specialized medical attention and emotional support.

    - Respite Care Homes: These homes offer short-term care services to individuals who require temporary assistance, usually because their regular caregiver is unavailable.

    Each type of care home offers unique services and support for different individual needs. It's important to research and consider all the options available before choosing the best care home for you or your loved one. Whichever the type of care home, Bellure HealthCare has professional caregivers, well-trained to fit into any of the care homes.
